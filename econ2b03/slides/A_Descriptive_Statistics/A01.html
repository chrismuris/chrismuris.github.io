Part I: Course Overview

What this course is about

  • During this course, you will learn the fundamentals of descriptive and inferential statistics
  • You will learn how to work with economic data using tools from statistics
  • Statistics provides a collection of methods for collecting, displaying, and analyzing data: descriptive statistics
  • Statistics provides a rigorous framework for drawing inference from data about a population: inferential statistics

By the end of this course

  • You will be able to summarize and visualize economic data with tables and graphs
  • You will be able to compute and interpret summary statistics
  • You will be able to use probability to quantify uncertainty
  • You will be able to construct and interpret confidence intervals and hypothesis tests
  • You will be able to estimate and interpret regression models in R
  • This matters because the methods you will learn are used widely
  • These methods guide business decisions and government policy
  • These methods are used across applied sciences
  • Even if you do not use them yourself, the people you work with will be using them

The audit study

  • To investigate, Oreopoulos (2011) presents results from an audit study of labor market outcomes
  • He conducts an experiment using randomized resumes sent to job postings
  • Fake resumes represented recent immigrants under the point system from China, India, and Pakistan
  • Fake resumes represented immigrants from Britain
  • Fake resumes represented non-immigrants with ethnic sounding names
  • Fake resumes represented non-immigrants without ethnic sounding names
  • Names and other characteristics were varied: education, job experience, immigration status, language proficiency, and more
  • These fake resumes were sent to randomly selected job ads
  • With few exceptions, four resumes were sent to each employer with randomized characteristics
  • For each submission, he records whether or not they were called back for an interview
  • The callback rate is the fraction of such resumes that receive an interview request
  1. Is there evidence for discrimination in the labor market?
  2. If so: should we combat it?
  3. If so: how?
